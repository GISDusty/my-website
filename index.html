My Journey

From Curiosity
to Career

I've always loved technology. Long before it became a career, it was curiosity—taking things apart, understanding systems, and wanting to know how everything connects. GIS became my professional entry point, but it has never been the boundary of my interests.

Growing up surveying with my father gave me an early appreciation for real-world systems and measurement—a natural path into GIS, where physical space and digital systems intersect. I built my career in the utility sector, and my work has since expanded into forestry, where environmental systems, long-term planning, and geospatial intelligence come together in meaningful ways.

Where physical space and digital systems intersect—that's where I do my best work.
